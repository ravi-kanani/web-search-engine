About Hair Romance

Hi, and welcome to Hair Romance, the site that’s all about loving your best accessory: your hair.

Here you’ll find tutorials, tips, tricks and beauty advice to help you to love your hair. Hair Romance posts hair news daily to bring you hair-spiration and ways to have fun with your hair.

You can find hair-spiration everywhere – from hair trends from fashion weeks in New York, Paris, Milan and Sydney to street-style hair and celebrity hair. Hair Romance breaks the trends down into easy hair tutorials, and shares hair product styling advice too. Come by every Friday for your dose of Big Hair and short hair on Short Cut Saturdays.

The hair behind Hair Romance

I’m Christina. I’m the hair behind Hair Romance.

Do you love your hair? I can tell you that, for years and years, I hated my hair. Then I had a haircut that changed my life.

I also met my husband in the same street that I met my hairdresser, and that’s where my two great romances began. I started this site in 2010 to share my Hair Romance and I blog with my husband at Mr & Mrs Romance.

You see, I used to fight with my hair and pretend that it wasn’t curly. I wanted it to be straight. My hair has a mind of its own and would rebel against every style I tried. Then I finally found a hairdresser who understood my hair. He cut it differently to anyone else and my hair looked amazing. I started to love my hair and it loved me back. It would stay in place, and would even let me straighten it every now and then.

I believe your hair is your best accessory. You wear your hair every day, and you should have fun with your hair.

You might think all this hair and beauty talk means I spend a lot of time on my hair but it’s not the case. The secret to good hair is a great haircut. It will save you time each morning as it will be easy to style and suit your lifestyle. All my tutorials are quick and usually work better in ‘second-day’ hair as I prefer to sleep in for 15 more minutes than to wash my hair.

I do all the hairstyles in my own hair and I take all the photos too. I’m not a hairdresser and I don’t have any special training, so if I can do it, I know you can too.

Some of my favourite go-to hairstyles are:

  • The classic Twist & Pin (be kind, it was my first video!)
  • Crown braided hairstyle
  • The giant top knot (which works even if you don’t have a lot of hair)
  • Side braided hairstyle (which features my pink ombre hair)
  • 5 ways to wear a scarf and a top knot
  • French roll Twist & Pin hairstyle
