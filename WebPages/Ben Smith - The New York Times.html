  10. BENEFITS AWARD DISPUTED

    A PERSONNEL policy that will enable the retiring City Manager here to collect $246,000 for unused sick leave and vacation time has infuriated taxpayers and led to an investigation of the legality of the payments. Joseph J. Squillace, who will retire Saturday after 40 years in city government, is to collect pay for 600 unused sick days and vacation days based on his final salary of more than $106,000 a year. In addition, Mr. Squillace will receive a $74,000 pension, the highest ever received by a New Jersey state or municipal official, according to the state's Division of Pensions. ''It's outrageous,'' said Harold Mrotzek, a resident of Willow Avenue, who threatened to bring a class-action suit against the city last month.

    By Ben Smith 3d
