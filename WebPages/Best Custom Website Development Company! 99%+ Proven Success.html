How long does it take to develop a website?

There are things we need our clients to provide before we can start developing a website. How quickly you respond and provide what is needed plays a big part in ensuring we work on your project on time. Under normal circumstances, a website will take 3-6 weeks to design depending on how many pages a client need.

Will my websites be mobile friendly?

Yes. We build websites that are mobile friendly. Our designers use a method known as responsive design which allows the sites to work well on a wide variety of devices. In a world where consumers are using mobile devices like smartphones and tablets, they can easily access your website through their devices.

What technologies do you normally build on?

  • WordPress
  • HubSpot
  • Drupal
  • Custom

Can I make changes to my site after it is completed?

Yes. Our web designers use open source technology to develop sites which allows clients with basic computer skills to make changes on their websites when they need to. However, our designers are always available to make changes for you if you need more complex changes to be done.

I have a website – does it need to be updated?

Technology changes so quickly today and if we don’t keep up with those changes, then your business can suffer. You may have been in business for a short time, or a number of years, but does your website still represent you properly? Are you fully up-to-date with the new GDPR law governing how you store and deal with a person’s data? If you have never heard of GDPR compliance, then you need our website development skills badly, just for this one issue!

What information do you need from me?

We will need a detailed brief from you, which will allow us to sketch, plan and create a mock-up design of your website. Once we have a plan that we feel represents your business ethos and goal, we can analyze what elements will work best for your business and communicate with you for approval. We will also need to know how you treat and protect customer data in order to comply with the new GDPR initiative.
