Latest City Faves, Stories, and a Clean Beauty Haul

March 13, 2020 By Heather Leave a Comment

Hi everyone, and Happy Friday!

How are you doing? (It’s a loaded question, I know.)

We made it to the end of the week … and it’s the 13th. I know I’m not alone in thinking that there are some scary things going on right now with the state of emergency the nation and world is in surrounding COVID-19 (outbreak of Coronavirus).

Things have certainly taken a turn around our neck of the woods and in New York City. Several of Scott’s upcoming work trips overseas have been canceled, my mom postponed her trip to come see us this weekend, and we ended up rescheduling Skyler’s birthday party until further notice. It’s a major bummer for sure.

I’m very grateful that no one I know directly has been physically affected, and I’m sending prayers to all of the families who have. Things have turned from an area of concern to panic, but we are doing our best to stay calm, stock up, and be indoors as much as possible.

The exception to all of this was a trip out to Manhattan yesterday to get to a casting call. We ran a few errands while we were out and decided to enjoy one last day before we put ourselves on voluntary lockdown.

(Skyler looks nervous in this picture! But he was loving our time together most of all.)

I think it’s important to acknowledge the idea of safety right now, but I would also like to provide a break from the madness on the Internet with today’s edition of Friday Favorites. Here’s hoping we all go into the weekend with calm hearts and educate ourselves when researching about it.

So, here’s what I’ve been loving and a few fun stories for you …

FRIDAY FAVORITES

  • Casting surprises

The picture you see at the top of this post was taken right after I had an impromptu casting call with Skyler and Scott yesterday. Ha!

The two of them planned to audition for a commercial together for a father and son role. And I was completely thrown off when the woman running the room said that I should just come in with them and cast all together for future projects. Um, what? So I did … with a messy bun, no lip gloss on, in tee-shirt and holy jeans, and everything else that would make me unprepared to take photos and film.

I guess the best part about situations like that is that you go into them with zero expectations at all. If I don’t get anything from it, no harm no foul!

It just goes to show that you never know where opportunities will arise.

  • The Bar Downstairs – Andez 5th Avenue

Scott had an errand to run at the NBA Store in Midtown, so we used the rest of the afternoon for time in the city together after the casting. We passed by a hotel that our friends stayed in a few years ago, and Scott reminded me that there is a really cool bar in the basement that you would never find without some guidance. And he was right. I barely found it while looking for it!

If you’re ever in the area and are looking for a unique atmosphere to grab premium drinks and appetizers, check out The Bar Downstairs at the Andaz 5th Avenue Hyatt. The service was great, and our waitress even brought Skyler a plate of fruit as a gift alongside his fries for dinner. (He ate off of our plates, too.) All great things to say!

  • Grace Street Coffee & Desserts

Speaking of places you should visit next time you’re near Koreatown, definitely curb your sweet tooth at Grace Street. It’s a huge cafeteria-style coffee and ice cream shop that blew our minds the first time we visited.

The menu is top notch, and it’s a one-of-a-kind Korean cafe that is more of an experience. They’re known for their Shaved Snows and Milk Teas, which we ordered last time. While they were fantastic, we ordered another suggestion from Yelp called the Milk Tea Boba Toast.

It was incredible. That is all.
