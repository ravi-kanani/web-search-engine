What’s in May 2019’s Degusta Box?

Motherhood Diaries unboxing this month’s Degusta Box below:

Products

MILKYBAR® MixUps Bag 32.5g – £0.65

“MILKYBAR® MIX-UPS is here! An exciting mix of MILKYBAR® white chocolate buttons with a cocoa filling and milk chocolate buttons with a milky filling. Our MILKYBAR® MIX-UPS are available in bags of different sizes. *You should receive two in your box. Available in major supermarkets and independent shops.”

Our Opinion – These are absolutely delicious and snack-sized, so perfect as little treats for kids. They are so moreish too it’s hard to stop at one packet!

5/5

Daelmans Duo Stroopwafels – £1.29

“Daelmans (Est. 1909) original Dutch stroopwafels. A tasty waffle and subtle caramel filling, made from the finest ingredients. The delicious treat with coffee, tea or just in between. *Available at Tesco, Sainsbury’s, Morrisons, Co-op or www.stroopwafels.com”

Our Opinion – My sister has moved to Amsterdam so when she visits us she brings back the genuine Dutch made Stroopwafels. These are the real deal and when you place them on top of your cup of something hot, the caramel oozes out of them and they raise the level of tasty snacks! Super sweet and probably not good for the sugar levels, but sometimes, you have to enjoy a delicious biscuit with your cuppa. Stroopwafels are the way to go!

5/5

Oteas Black Tea & Apricot – £3.75 – PRODUCT OF THE MONTH

“This black tea with apricot is infused with sweet and nutty apricots to create a light fruity beverage. Using only high-quality black tea and apricot pieces responsibly sourced, all delivered in a fully biodegradable bag. Enjoy many of our teas hot or cold.”

Our Opinion – I can’t try this tea at the moment because I am pregnant, but I offered it to a friend and asked her to rate what she thought of it. She said that she could taste the infusion of apricot, and it would make for a nice cup of black tea on occasion. I can’t wait to try it myself – I am a fierce tea lover, but I have to resort to decaf tea at the moment

4/5

KOKO Dairy Free SUPER Milk – £1.99

“Koko Dairy Free Super Milk is enriched with nine key vitamins and minerals, offering especially boosted nutrition for all ages from toddlers to seniors. It provides more nutrients than any regular plant or dairy milk! With a creamy, full-bodied taste, it’s delicious to use as a full-fat milk replacement. *Available at Tesco, Sainsbury’s, Morrisons, Waitrose, Ocado, Co-op.”

Our Opinion – These were absolutely delicious and if the boys didn’t drink milk like water and this Koko Dairy Free milk wasn’t as expensive, I would replace the milk in the fridge with this brand. I love it! The milk almost tastes like a milkshake. I’d love to try frothing this milk in my coffee machine and see how it comes out.

5/5

SHORE Seaweed Puffs – £1.19

“‘SHORE takes super healthy seaweed and makes nutritious and tasty vegan snacks. SHORE seaweed Puffs are a deliciously crunchy, unami filled savoury snack that’s packed with good for you nutrition and all for unde 100 calories per bag and available in 4 fantastic flavours. *You should receive 1 of 4 flavours in your box. Available online at Ocado, Wholefoods & As Nature Intended stores.”

Our Opinion – These seaweeds puffs are light and full of flavour. The boys weren’t too enthusiastic about these, but I loved them and would love to fit one in my bag for when I feel peckish. Sweet & Smoky was lovely!

4/5

La Gioiosa Prosecco Brut DOC Treviso – £3.49

“Family-owned La Gioiosa is one of Italy’s leading Prosecco producers. Based in Treviso in the heart of ‘Prosecco country’, it produces quality wines. Its name pays tribute to the old definition of the Treviso province, ‘Marc Gioiosa at amorosa’ (joyous and Amorous March) underlining the sociable Italian way of life. *Available at Ocado, Budgens, McColls, Nisa Locally, Costcutter.”

Our Opinion – I didn’t try this Prosecco as I am pregnant, but my husband did, and he said it tastes like a nice quality bottle of Prosecco. He is not a fan of fizzy alcohol, but he would understand why others would like this as a favourable bottle of Prosecco.

3.5/5

Firefly Drinks Lemon, Lime & Ginger – £1.55

“Our revitalising botanical drinks are expertly crafted from still water, fruit juice and active botanicals for the perfect blend of flavour and refreshment. We never add anything nasty – all our drinks contain naturally occurring sugars, no colours or preservatives and are Vegan-friendly. Simply sit, sip and smile! *Available at Waitrose and Ocado.”

Our Opinion – I love anything vegan, natural and full of lemon and lime, so I found this drink super delicious. The spice from the ginger complemented the lemon and lime and if drunk ice cold, this is would be a great refreshing drink for the summer.

5/5

Bravura Foods Free From Fellows – £1.99

“Free From Fellows Vegan Midget Gems are made with a delicious twist – they are made without sugar, gluten, gelatine and dairy! These products are suitable for vegans and vegetarians. They are also egg free, soy free, GMO-free, nut free, and are free from artificial flavours and colours. * Available at Ocado, Boots, Sainsbury’s, Holland & Barrett, Morrison’s and all leading health food stores.”

Our Opinion – These Midget Gems (which taste like wine gums) are free from everything! I am surprised that Bravura Foods managed to keep them so sweet and tasty. Perfect for people with religious preferences/allergies.

4/5

Fruity Beauties Organic & Fairtrade Snack Bars – £1.50

“Fruity Beauties are on a mission to provide a fun and nutritious burst of life. Our delicious Fairtrade and Organic bars are high in fibre and one of your five-a-day. Positively juicy, these bars are traceable farm to fork and promote sustainability. Great for tea breaks and lunch boxes alike! *Available at Ocado and via direct contact.”

Our Opinion – I love providing my boys with healthy sweet snacks after school, and these were great. The boys commented that they tasted sweet and delicious, and they wanted to come back for more. The Fruity Beauties are quite expensive at £1.50, so I probably wouldn’t buy these again. But I do love that there is more focus on kids’ healthy snacks rather than the high sugar, high-fat chocolate/sweet snacks that are so readily available in the shops now.

3.5/5

Indie Bay Snacks Spelt Pretzel Bites with Rock Salt – £1

“Indie Bay Snacks have reinvented the classic pretzel, using new ingredients and processes to create a snack with benefits. The crunchy, delicious pearls are sources of protein and fibre that contain fewer than 100 calories per portion, and they’re vegan too! *You should receive two items in your box. Available at Sainsbury’s, Ocado, Amazon & others.”

Our Opinion – These were simple, slightly salted pretzel snacks which tasted delicious, but they gave me a dry mouth. They’re great snacks, but be prepared to feel very thirsty afterwards!

What did I like most about May 2019’s Degusta Box?

It was great to see healthier alternatives to sugary snacks, and I loved Koko Dairy free milk. I hope it’s easily accessible in the shops!

Rating:

4 out of 5 – Great mixed bag of ingredients, which I’d love to try again!
