the ability to share a ventilator amongst multiple patients that is the constraint that is hit first. by allocating one ventilator to one average-size adult male patient, over 65% of the machine‚Äôs capacity to ventilate is not being used. from a scaling capacity standpoint, this is inefficient. now, it turns out that the ability to use a ‚Äúsplitter‚Äù to route the air pumping capacity of a work? short answer: yes. they used standard equipment found in an emergency room to create a two-way and four-way splitter. they then used the ventilator to ventilate four simulated-lung devices for patients. you‚Äôre in volume control, set at 1000ml for a presumptive 500ml per patient. what if one of those et tubes becomes linked? or occluded? now that entire 1000ml gets delivered to one
