guidance no categories uncategorized product management methodologies: agile vs waterfall vs scrum vs kanban product managers face a common challenge in the nascent stages of creating a product: finding the ideal methodology.. while there’s a wide assortment of methodologies to choose from, each one has its own principles, rules, practices, processes, and pros and cons. and, there’s no such thing as a one-size-fits-all approach. the project management institute (pmi) defines product management methodology as a “framework that is used to structure, plan, and control the process of developing an information system.” think of these methodologies as a series of steps that a product manager needs to execute (with the help of a team, of course ) to design, develop, and test a product toward its successful launch/ release. while it shares a certain degree of overlap with project management, product management is restricted to managing the life cycles of products only – from their birth (ideation/ design phase) to their distribution (market launch/ release). however, with so many product management methodologies to choose from, how do you cherry-pick the best one for your product? the short answer is — it depends. the right methodology depends entirely on the variables you’re working with – primarily your timelines, flexibility, and budget. there’s no one-size-fits-all approach, there’s also not just one approach that’ll fit the bill. you may find that a combination of methodologies, such as agile + waterfall, may be right for your team. continue reading
