Thieves Party In San Mateo Co. Restaurant Closed During Coronavirus Shelter-In-Place

March 27, 2020 at 7:19 am
Filed Under:Break-in, Burglary, Coronavirus, COVID-19, Half Moon Bay, La Costanera, Montara, San Mateo County, Theft


MONTARA, San Mateo County (KPIX 5) – A restaurant along the San Mateo County coast closed due to the coronavirus shelter-in-place was trashed by thieves who partied at his business, the owner said.

The break-in took place at La Costanera, a Peruvian restaurant in Montara, over the weekend. Surveillance footage showed at least four suspects breaking in, stealing cash and drinking plenty of alcohol. The thieves did not leave until the following day.

Surveillance footage of a break-in at La Costanera restaurant in Montara. (CBS)

Manager Jorge Palacios said damage aside, the most important thing right now is for everyone to be safe.

“Be safe, it doesn’t matter if it’s a restaurant or your home – people are gonna be looking for it,” Palacios said. “We’re on week one and we don’t even see the entire problems yet.”

The restaurant, which has been closed since March 16th, plans to reopen once the lockdown is lifted.
